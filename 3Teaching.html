Overview

I teach at USC as a Graduate Teaching Assistant (TA). The main responsibility as a TA in linguistics is to lead discussions and teach handy skills for language study. The courses that I have taught as a TA at USC include Language and Society and Language and Mind. I have also TA'ed for Introduction to Linguistics at East China Normal University.

I have completed various teacher training courses, including the Teacher's Education Program (Tianjin Normal University) and Teaching Practicum (Department of Linguistics, USC).

Language and Society (LING 115)

  • Undergraduate General Education (TA'ed in Fall 2020, 2018, 2017, 2016; Spring 2019, 2018.)
  • Instructors: Prof. Sandra Disner and Prof. Andrew Simpson.

Language and Society is a course that looks into how language and society interact with and influence each other. Below is one of my favorate quotes that I always shared with students during the first meeting:

... the development of language must be counted as by far the most important evolutionary development in the human species. And once man’s survival and preeminence had been assured through language, it was language that made possible our living in larger stable and more viable communities, followed ultimately by the emergence of language-based intellectual, moral and legal systems of rational thought, literature, song, and drama, such as are the glories of civilized life.(R.H. Robins, General Linguistics, 4th edition, p.17)

Responsibility

  • Prepared materials for discussion sections
  • Taught discussion sections
  • Graded assignments and exams
  • Provided academic assistance to students

Main topics:

  • Dialect and language
  • Languages of special roles
  • Language preservation and revitalization
  • Pidgin and Creole
  • Bilingualism
  • Language relativity
  • Language and gender
