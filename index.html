About Me

About Me

I am Ph.D. Candidate in linguistics at the University of Southern California. I study phonology, the linguistic knowledge of sound patterns, and I explore to what extent this knowledge can be economically represented and computed in the human mind.

My research projects involve various Chinese languages/dialects and an under-threat Romance language, Ladin. The data are from my own fieldwork and/or existing corpus.

Apart from theoretical linguistics, I am also enthusiastic about sociolinguistics. I worked as a teaching assistant at USC for the course Language and Society between 2016 and 2020.

Here is how to pronounce my name in Mandarin Chinese: [i˥.fan˧˥.jɑŋ˧˥].

Research Interests

General
  • Phonology and its interface with morphology
  • Phonetics
Specific (Past projects)
  • Identity effect in phonology-reduplication interaction
  • Phonetics and phonology of understudied languages
  • Gestural coordination of diphthongs
  • Rime change patterns of Chinese languages/dialects
  • Morpheme realization and the architecture of grammar

I am a member of USC Phonetics and Phonology Group  and Language and Computation Lab (ShLAC).
