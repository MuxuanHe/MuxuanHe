About Me

I am a Ph.D. student (ABD) in linguistics at the University of Southern California. I study phonology, the linguistic knowledge of sound patterns, and I explore to what extent this knowledge can be economically represented and computed in the human mind.

My current and past research projects involve a range of typologically and geographically diverse languages, including various Chinese languages/dialects, Austronesian languages, and an under-threat Romance language, Ladin, based on my own fieldwork data and/or existing corpus data.

I am a member of USC Phonetics and Phonology Group  and Language and Computation Lab (ShLAC).
